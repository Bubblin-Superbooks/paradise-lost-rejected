Sleepst thou Companion dear, what sleep can closeThy eye-lids? and remembrest what DecreeOf yesterday, so late hath past the lipsOf Heav’ns Almightie. Thou to me thy thoughtsWast wont, I mine to thee was wont to impart;Both waking we were one; how then can nowThy sleep dissent? new Laws thou seest impos’d;New Laws from him who reigns, new minds may raiseIn us who serve, new Counsels, to debateWhat doubtful may ensue, more in this placeTo utter is not safe.
