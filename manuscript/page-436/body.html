MeanwhileTo resalute the World with sacred LightLeucothea wak’d, and with fresh dews imbalmdThe Earth, when Adam and first Matron EveHad ended now thir Orisons, and found,Strength added from above, new hope to springOut of despaire, joy, but with fear yet linkt;Which thus to Eve his welcome words renewd.

Eve, easily may Faith admit, that allThe good which we enjoy, from Heav’n descendsBut that from us ought should ascend to Heav’nSo prevalent as to concerne the mindOf God high blest, or to incline his will,Hard to belief may seem; yet this will Prayer,Or one short sigh of humane breath, up-borneEv’n to the Seat of God.
