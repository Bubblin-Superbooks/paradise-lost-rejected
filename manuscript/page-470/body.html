O thou that future things canst representAs present, Heav’nly instructer, I reviveAt this last sight, assur’d that Man shall liveWith all the Creatures, and thir seed preserve.Farr less I now lament for one whole WorldOf wicked Sons destroyd, then I rejoyceFor one Man found so perfet and so just,That God voutsafes to raise another WorldFrom him, and all his anger to forget.But say, what mean those colourd streaks in Heavn,Distended as the Brow of God appeas’d,Or serve they as a flourie verge to bindeThe fluid skirts of that same watrie
