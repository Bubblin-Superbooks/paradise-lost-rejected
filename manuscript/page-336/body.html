Love, then which perhaps no blissEnjoy’d by us excites his envie more;Or this, or worse, leave not the faithful sideThat gave thee being, stil shades thee and protects.The Wife, where danger or dishonour lurks,Safest and seemliest by her Husband staies,Who guards her, or with her the worst endures.

To whom the Virgin Majestie of Eve, As one who loves, and some unkindness meets,With sweet austeer composure thus reply’d.

Ofspring of Heav’n and Earth, and all Earths Lord,That such an enemie we have, who seeksOur ruin, both by thee informd I learne,And from the parting Angel over-heardAs in a shadie nook I stood behind,Just then returnd at shut of Evening Flours.But that thou shouldst my firmness therefore doubtTo God or thee, because we have a foeMay tempt it, I expected not to hear.His violence thou fearst not, being such,As wee, not capable of death or paine,Can either not
