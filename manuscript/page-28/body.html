For Spirits when they pleaseCan either Sex assume, or both; so softAnd uncompounded is their Essence pure,Not ti’d or manacl’d with joynt or limb,Nor founded on the brittle strength of bones,Like cumbrous flesh; but in what shape they chooseDilated or condens’t, bright or obscure,Can execute their aerie purposes,And works of love or enmity fulfill.For those the Race of Israel oft forsookTheir living strength, and unfrequented leftHis righteous Altar, bowing lowly downTo bestial Gods; for which their heads as lowBow’d down in Battel, sunk
