Which of those rebell Spirits adjudg’d to HellCom’st thou, escap’d thy prison, and transform’d,Why satst thou like an enemie in waiteHere watching at the head of these that sleep?

Know ye not then said Satan, filld with scorn,Know ye not me? ye knew me once no mateFor you, there sitting where ye durst not soare;Not to know mee argues your selves unknown,The lowest of your throng; or if ye know,Why ask ye, and superfluous beginYour message, like to end as much in vain?To whom thus Zephon, answering scorn with scorn.Think not, revolted Spirit, thy shape the same,Or undiminisht brightness, to be knownAs when thou stoodst in Heav’n upright and pure;That Glorie then, when thou no more wast
