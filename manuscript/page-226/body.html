O Heav’n! that such resemblance of the HighestShould yet remain, where faith and realtieRemain not; wherfore should not strength & mightThere fail where Vertue fails, or weakest proveWhere boldest; though to sight unconquerable?His puissance, trusting in th’ Almightie’s aide,I mean to try, whose Reason I have tri’dUnsound and false; nor is it aught but just,That he who in debate of Truth hath won,Should win in Arms, in both disputes alikeVictor; though brutish that contest and foule,When Reason hath to deal with force, yet soMost reason is that Reason overcome.
