He ceas’d; and Satan staid not to reply,But glad that now his Sea should find a shore,With fresh alacritie and force renew’dSprings upward like a Pyramid of fireInto the wilde expanse, and through the shockOf fighting Elements, on all sides roundEnviron’d wins his way; harder besetAnd more endanger’d, then when Argo pass’dThrough Bosporus betwixt the justling Rocks:Or when Ulysses on the Larbord shunndCharybdis, and by th’ other whirlpool steard.So he with difficulty and labour hardMov’d on, with difficulty and labour
