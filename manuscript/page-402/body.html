Ye have th’ accountOf my performance: What remaines, ye Gods,But up and enter now into full bliss.

So having said, a while he stood, expectingThir universal shout and high applauseTo fill his eare, when contrary he hearsOn all sides, from innumerable tonguesA dismal universal hiss, the soundOf public scorn; he wonderd, but not longHad leasure, wondring at himself now more;His Visage drawn he felt to sharp and spare,His Armes clung to his Ribs, his Leggs entwiningEach other, till supplanted down he fellA monstrous Serpent on his Belly prone,Reluctant, but in
