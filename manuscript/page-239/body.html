And now thir mightiest quelld, the battel swerv’d,With many an inrode gor’d; deformed routEnter’d, and foul disorder; all the groundWith shiverd armour strow’n, and on a heapChariot and Charioter lay overturndAnd fierie foaming Steeds; what stood, recoyldOrewearied, through the faint Satanic HostDefensive scarse, or with pale fear surpris’d,Then first with fear surpris’d and sense of paineFled ignominious, to such evil broughtBy sinne of disobedience, till that hourNot liable to fear or flight or paine.Far otherwise th’ inviolable SaintsIn Cubic Phalanx firm advanc’t entire,Invulnerable, impenitrably arm’d:Such high advantages thir innocenceGave them above thir foes, not to have sinnd,Not to have disobei’d; in fight they stoodUnwearied, unobnoxious to be
