So seem’dFarr off the flying Fiend: at last appeerHell bounds high reaching to the horrid Roof,And thrice threefold the Gates; three folds were BrassThree Iron, three of Adamantine Rock,Impenitrable, impal’d with circling fire,Yet unconsum’d. Before the Gates there satOn either side a formidable shape;The one seem’d Woman to the waste, and fair,But ended foul in many a scaly fouldVoluminous and vast, a Serpent arm’dWith mortal sting: about her middle roundA cry of Hell Hounds never ceasing bark’dWith wide Cerberean mouths full loud, and rungA hideous Peal: yet, when they list,
