Out of my sight, thou Serpent, that name bestBefits thee with him leagu’d, thy self as falseAnd hateful; nothing wants, but that thy shape,Like his, and colour Serpentine may shewThy inward fraud, to warn all Creatures from theeHenceforth; least that too heav’nly form, pretendedTo hellish falshood, snare them. But for theeI had persisted happie, had not thy prideAnd wandring vanitie, when lest was safe,Rejected my forewarning, and disdain’dNot to be trusted, longing to be seenThough by the Devil himself, him overweeningTo over-reach, but with the Serpent meetingFool’d and beguil’d, by him thou, I by thee,To trust thee from my side, imagin’d wise,Constant, mature, proof against all assaults,And understood not all was but a shewRather then solid vertu, all but a RibCrooked by nature, bent, as now appears,More to
