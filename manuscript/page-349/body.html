So gloz’d the Tempter, and his Proem tun’d;Into the Heart of Eve his words made way,Though at the voice much marveling; at lengthNot unamaz’d she thus in answer spake.What may this mean? Language of Man pronounc’tBy Tongue of Brute, and human sense exprest?The first at lest of these I thought deni’dTo Beasts, whom God on their Creation-DayCreated mute to all articulat sound;The latter I demurre, for in thir looksMuch reason, and in thir actions oft appeers.Thee, Serpent, suttlest beast of all the fieldI knew, but not with human voice endu’d;Redouble then this miracle, and say,How cam’st thou speakable of mute, and howTo me so friendly grown above the restOf brutal kind, that daily are in sight?Say, for such wonder claims attention due.
