O fairest of Creation, last and bestOf all Gods Works, Creature in whom excell’dWhatever can to fight or thought be found,Holy, divine, good, amiable, or sweet! How art thou lost, how on a sudden lost,Defac’t, deflourd, and now to Death devote?Rather how hast thou yeelded to transgressThe strict forbiddance, how to violateThe sacred Fruit forbidd’n! som cursed fraudOf Enemie hath beguil’d thee, yet unknown,And mee with thee hath ruind, for with theeCertain my resolution is to Die;How can I live without thee, how forgoeThy sweet Converse and Love so dearly joyn’d,To live again in these wilde Woods forlorn?Should God create another Eve, and IAnother Rib afford, yet loss of theeWould never from my heart; no no, I feelThe Link
