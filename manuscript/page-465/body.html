O Visions ill foreseen! better had ILiv’d ignorant of future, so had borneMy part of evil onely, each dayes lotAnough to bear; those now, that were dispenstThe burd’n of many Ages, on me lightAt once, by my foreknowledge gaining BirthAbortive, to torment me ere thir being,With thought that they must be.
