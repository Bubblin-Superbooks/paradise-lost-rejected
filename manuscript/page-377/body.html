To whom then first incenst Adam repli’d.Is this the Love, is the recompenceOf mine to thee, ingrateful Eve, exprestImmutable when thou wert lost, not I,Who might have liv’d and joyd immortal bliss,Yet willingly chose rather Death with thee:And am I now upbraided, as the causeOf thy transgressing? not enough severe,It seems, in thy restraint: what could I more?I warn’d thee, I admonish’d thee, foretoldThe danger, and the lurking EnemieThat lay in wait; beyond this had bin force,And force upon free Will hath here no place.But confidence then bore thee on, secureEither to meet no danger, or to findeMatter of glorious trial; and perhapsI also err’d in
