To bow and sue for graceWith suppliant knee, and deifie his powerWho from the terrour of this Arm so lateDoubted his Empire, that were low indeed,That were an ignominy and shame beneathThis downfall; since by Fate the strength of GodsAnd this Empyreal substance cannot fail,Since through experience of this great eventIn Arms not worse, in foresight much advanc’t,We may with more successful hope resolveTo wage by force or guile eternal WarrIrreconcileable, to our grand Foe,Who now triumphs, and in th’ excess of joySole reigning holds the Tyranny of Heav’n.

So spake th’ Apostate Angel, though in pain,Vaunting aloud, but rackt with deep despare:And him thus answer’d soon his bold Compeer.
