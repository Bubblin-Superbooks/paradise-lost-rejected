More destroy’d then thusWe should be quite abolisht and expire.What fear we then? what doubt we to incenseHis utmost ire? which to the highth enrag’d,Will either quite consume us, and reduceTo nothing this essential, happier farrThen miserable to have eternal being:Or if our substance be indeed Divine,And cannot cease to be, we are at worstOn this side nothing; and by proof we feelOur power sufficient to disturb his Heav’n,And with perpetual inrodes to Allarme,Though inaccessible, his fatal Throne:Which if not Victory is yet Revenge.
