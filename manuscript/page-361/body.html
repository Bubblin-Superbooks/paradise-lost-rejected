Experience, next to thee I owe,Best guide; not following thee, I had remaindIn ignorance, thou op’nst Wisdoms way,And giv’st access, though secret she retire.And I perhaps am secret; Heav’n is high,High and remote to see from thence distinctEach thing on Earth; and other care perhapsMay have diverted from continual watchOur great Forbidder, safe with all his SpiesAbout him. But to Adam in what sortShall I appeer? shall I to him make knownAs yet my change, and give him to partakeFull happiness with mee, or rather not,But keep the odds of Knowledge in my powerWithout Copartner? so to add what wantsIn Femal Sex, the more to draw his Love,And render me more equal, and perhapsA thing not undesireable, somtimeSuperior; for inferior who is free?This may be well: but what if God have seen,And Death ensue? then I shall be no more,And Adam
