To suffer, as to doe,Our strength is equal, nor the Law unjustThat so ordains: this was at first resolv’d,If we were wise, against so great a foeContending, and so doubtful what might fall.I laugh, when those who at the Spear are boldAnd vent’rous, if that fail them, shrink and fearWhat yet they know must follow, to endureExile, or ignominy, or bonds, or pain,The sentence of thir Conquerour: This is nowOur doom; which if we can sustain and bear,Our Supream Foe in time may much remitHis anger, and perhaps thus farr remov’dNot mind us not offending, satisfi’dWith what is punish’t; whence these raging firesWill slack’n, if his breath stir not thir flames.Our purer essence then will overcomeThir noxious vapour, or enur’d not feel,Or chang’d at length, and to the place
