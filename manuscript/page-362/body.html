So saying, from the Tree her step she turnd,But first low Reverence don, as to the powerThat dwelt within, whose presence had infus’dInto the plant sciential sap, deriv’dFrom Nectar, drink of Gods. Adam the whileWaiting desirous her return, had woveOf choicest Flours a Garland to adorneHer Tresses, and her rural labours crownAs Reapers oft are wont thir Harvest Queen.Great joy he promis’d to his thoughts, and newSolace in her return, so long delay’d;Yet oft his heart, divine of somthing ill,Misgave him; hee the faultring measure felt;And forth to meet her went, the way she tookThat Morn when first they parted; by the TreeOf Knowledge he must pass, there he her
