Up he rodeFollowd with acclamation and the soundSymphonious of ten thousand Harpes that tun’dAngelic harmonies: the Earth, the AireResounded, (thou remember’st, for thou heardst)The Heav’ns and all the Constellations rung,The Planets in thir stations list’ning stood,While the bright Pomp ascended jubilant.Open, ye everlasting Gates, they sung,Open, ye Heav’ns, your living dores; let inThe great Creator from his work returndMagnificent, his Six days work, a World;Open, and henceforth oft; for God will deigneTo visit oft the dwellings of just MenDelighted, and with frequent intercourseThither will send his winged MessengersOn errands of supernal Grace. So sungThe glorious Train
