Accuse not Nature, she hath don her part;Do thou but thine, and be not diffidentOf Wisdom, she deserts thee not, if thouDismiss not her, when most thou needst her nigh,By attributing overmuch to thingsLess excellent, as thou thy self perceav’st.For what admir’st thou, what transports thee so,An outside? fair no doubt, and worthy wellThy cherishing, thy honouring, and thy love,Not thy subjection: weigh with her thy self;Then value: Oft times nothing profits moreThen self-esteem, grounded on just and rightWell manag’d; of that skill the more thou know’st,The more she will acknowledge thee her Head,And to realities yeild all her
