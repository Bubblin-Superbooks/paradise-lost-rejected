Sun’s lucent OrbeThrough his glaz’d Optic Tube yet never saw.The place he found beyond expression bright,Compar’d with aught on Earth, Medal or Stone;Not all parts like, but all alike informdWith radiant light, as glowing Iron with fire;If mettal, part seemd Gold, part Silver cleer;If stone, Carbuncle most or Chrysolite,Rubie or Topaz, to the Twelve that shonIn Aarons Brest-plate, and a stone besidesImagind rather oft then elsewhere seen,That stone, or like to that which here belowPhilosophers in vain so long have sought,In vain, though by thir powerful Art they bindeVolatil Hermes, and call up unboundIn various shapes old Proteus from the Sea,Draind through a Limbec to his Native forme.What wonder then if fields and regions hereBreathe forth Elixir pure, and Rivers runPotable Gold, when with one vertuous touchTh’ Arch-chimic Sun so farr from us remoteProduces with Terrestrial Humor mixtHere in the dark so many
