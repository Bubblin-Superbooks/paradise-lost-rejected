The key of this infernal Pit by due,And by command of Heav’ns all-powerful KingI keep, by him forbidden to unlockThese Adamantine Gates; against all forceDeath ready stands to interpose his dart,Fearless to be o’rematcht by living might.But what ow I to his commands aboveWho hates me, and hath hither thrust me downInto this gloom of Tartarus profound,To sit in hateful Office here confin’d,Inhabitant of Heav’n, and heav’nlie-born,Here in perpetual agonie and pain,With terrors and with clamors compasst roundOf mine own brood, that on my bowels feed:Thou art my Father, thou my Author, thouMy being gav’st me; whom should I obeyBut thee, whom follow? thou wilt bring me soonTo that new world of light and bliss, amongThe Gods who live at ease, where I shall ReignAt thy right hand voluptuous, as beseemsThy daughter
