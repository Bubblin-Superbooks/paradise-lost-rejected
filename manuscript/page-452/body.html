Immediately a placeBefore his eyes appeard, sad, noysom, dark,A Lazar-house it seemd, wherein were laidNumbers of all diseas’d, all maladiesOf gastly Spasm, or racking torture, qualmesOf heart-sick Agonie, all feavorous kinds,Convulsions, Epilepsies, fierce Catarrhs,Intestin Stone and Ulcer, Colic pangs,Dropsies, and Asthma’s, and Joint-racking Rheums.Dire was the tossing, deep the groans, despairTended the sick busiest from Couch to Couch;And over them triumphant Death his DartShook, but delaid to strike, though oft invok’tWith vows, as thir chief good, and final hope.Sight so deform what heart of Rock could longDrie-ey’d behold?
