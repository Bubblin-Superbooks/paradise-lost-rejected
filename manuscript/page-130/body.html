O For that warning voice, which he who sawTh’ Apocalyps, heard cry in Heaven aloud,Then when the Dragon, put to second rout,Came furious down to be reveng’d on men,Wo to the Inhabitants on Earth! that now,While time was, our first Parents had bin warndThe coming of thir secret foe, and scap’dHaply so scap’d his mortal snare; for nowSatan, now first inflam’d with rage, came down,The Tempter ere th’ Accuser of man-kind,To wreck on innocent frail man his lossOf that first Battel, and his flight to Hell:Yet not rejoycing in his speed, though bold,Far off and fearless, nor with cause to boast,Begins his dire attempt, which nigh the birthNow rowling, boiles in his tumultuous brest,And like a devillish Engine back recoilesUpon himself; horror and doubt distractHis troubl’d
