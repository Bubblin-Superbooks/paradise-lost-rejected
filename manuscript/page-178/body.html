Book V.

Now Morn her rosie steps in th’ Eastern ClimeAdvancing, sow’d the Earth with Orient Pearle,When Adam wak’t, so customd, for his sleepWas Aerie light, from pure digestion bred,And temperat vapors bland, which th’ only soundOf leaves and fuming rills, Aurora’s fan,Lightly dispers’d, and the shrill Matin SongOf Birds on every bough; so much the moreHis wonder was to find unwak’nd EveWith Tresses discompos’d, and glowing Cheek,As through unquiet rest: he on his sideLeaning half-rais’d, with looks of cordial LoveHung over her enamour’d, and beheldBeautie, which whether waking or asleep,Shot forth peculiar Graces; then with voiceMilde, as when Zephyrus on Flora breathes,Her hand soft touching, whisperd thus.
