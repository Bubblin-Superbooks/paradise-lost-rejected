So spake the Prince of Angels; to whom thusThe Adversarie. Nor think thou with windOf airie threats to aw whom yet with deedsThou canst not. Hast thou turnd the least of theseTo flight, or if to fall, but that they riseUnvanquisht, easier to transact with meeThat thou shouldst hope, imperious, & with threatsTo chase me hence? erre not that so shall endThe strife which thou call’st evil, but wee styleThe strife of Glorie: which we mean to win,Or turn this Heav’n it self into the HellThou fablest, here however to dwell free,If not to reign: mean while thy utmost force,And join him nam’d Almightie to thy aid,I flie not, but have sought thee farr and nigh.
